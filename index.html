Which channels should are enabled - only these channels have the protection features and will turn on


The Battery Type is the chemistry of your battery. It determines mostly nominal, over and under voltage of your battery.
How many of the cells of your selected chemistry your battery pack has in series.
The Capacity of your Battery pack in Ah (total battery, not just a single cell)
The cell voltage at which an over voltage protection event is fired.
The cell voltage at which an under voltage protection event is fired.
Under voltage Protection voltage for the whole battery pack.
Over voltage Protection voltage for the whole battery pack.
Max output (discharge) current of a single connected channel - if you want 300A combined output current and have all 3 channels enabled, this should be 100A
Max input (charge) current of a single connected channel - if you want 99A combined charging current and have all 3 channels enabled, this should be 33A
The difference in current that can flow between the batteries which will trigger a protection event. With identical battery packs this current may be caused by different wire lengths or different stages of degradation inside the battery. The difference of course applies to both charging and discharging and "idling"
The current spike sensitivity changes the instant over current (short) protection value. If this is set too high, a short may be detected too late or not at all. If you encounter short Faults under high loads at low speeds, your drivetrain may have a current spike problem, which means that the motor controller is seeking really high currents for short durations and is thereby potentially harming the battery. You can just turn down the sensitivity to avoid the faults, we would however advise you to look at other options of avoiding spikes (additional filter capacitors near the motor controller).
The load capacitance value is the input capacitance on your load's power connection. The value is used to estimate weather the right load or even any load is connected. If this setting is grossly wrong, it may lead to faults "nothing connected" faults.
The shunt temperature is a good representation of how warm the power stage of the VoltaicBMS is. You may set the temperature as you like if you need this BMS to be below a certain temperature As with the precharge transistor, setting this max temp lower might let you catch anomalies in your setup before it's too late.
With this setting, you decide if the BMS should automatically try to turn on the channels on power up.
Idle time before BMS automatically turns off. Idle is defined as either not in operation mode or <0.5A current drawn from each battery. Resets every time idle state is left.
The number of times, the configuration has been updated. This mostly serves as a confirmation, that all of your changes have been correctly sent and read back - if you save the changes, this number should increase by 1.

VoltaicBMS Interface

Version:

VoltaicBMS Interface is a program for interfacing with the Voltaic VoltaicBMS System. It both acts as a Monitor for live data, a data logger for later analysis and a configuration tool to make the BMS fit your needs.

To really make the interface feel like your own, use the config:

(to make the color changes visible, go toggle "ColorMode" until you see the changes)

As this is a progressive web app, the newest version is basically just a swipe down away. As we found this feature to be a bit distracting in everyday use, you can enable the temporary swipe down here.

Note: The swipe down updates are only temporary and will be forgotten once you restart the app. For permanent updates, confirm the update in the "new version" popup.

Note:
I would kindly ask you to be a bit gentle as it's still a pre-release version that needs more development and testing. If you have severe problems or want to make a usability friendly suggestion, feel free to contact me!
